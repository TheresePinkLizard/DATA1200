PAOLO MATTEUZZI

STUDIO Zaha Hadid Architects

Just as in the design of a supercar, high-tech architecture is always pushing the boundaries, in terms of both stylis- tic research and the conception and use of revolutionary materials. Zaha Hadid Architects (ZHA) has been promot- ing innovation and new codes for years without ever forgetting the demands of tradition. The future development of techniques for the construction of com- plex architectures will hardly be able to avoid drawing on projects such as the Morpheus Hotel in Macao of the Leeza SOHO office tower in Beijing. The lat- ter not only features the tallest atrium in history, but also a fascinating twisting movement that optimizes the exposure of the façade. The Morpheus Hotel, on the other hand, represents the highest point of parametric research and owes its formal qualities to computational design tools in conjunction with robotic pro- duction methods. This research opened up the possibility of radically surpassing the Fordist model linked to mass produc- tion, with almost identical buildings, to arrive at the customization of the prod- uct, even on a large scale. Today, the search for aesthetic perfec- tion that pursues beauty as an absolute value has definitively waned compared to the value that all the components of the project, together, must achieve. If it is true that beauty will save the world, then it is the concept of beauty itself that must be investigated. The transformation of cities will soon be linked to mobility infrastructures that are no longer physical, but media-based. And the cars of the future will also have to adapt to it. The freedom to drive one´s own vehicle is driverless era, cars will be controlled by an algorithm, and it will become more and more the privilege of the few to actually get into a car, put their hands on the wheel and drive aim- lessly, desiring only that the car gives us the most exciting and immersive experi- ence.
